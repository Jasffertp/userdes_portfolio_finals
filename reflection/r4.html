R4: INFORMATION ARCHITECTURE

The topic was very interesting because it is not included in our syllabus however, it helpedus students to foster a good and proper website design. It was not only used for our personal websites but also for the soon-to-be websites or applications of our group. It guided me to think better and really helped me to understand the user more. The topic that was most impactful to me and I think I might be using is the ‘Trunk test’, which basically you imagine a scenario wherein you are put in a trunk, blindfolded, and are the car drives around town, you’ll be dropped off in a place you don’t know and you’ll need to know how to go home, the ways of going home are finding landmarks that are close to your home. It was really useful and it can be implemented in terms of designing a website by putting you in the perspective of the person in the trunk, imagining that you are lost in your website and you do not know how to get back home, so you put landmarks or sections in your header or sidebar of the website to get back to the homepage of the website where all the landmarks and sites can be seen. I did not know that something so simple is so good and efficient at explaining how to design a website, I would definitely implement that in my website and it really did help me increase my understanding on how to properly design a website, not only for the sake of designing it, but for the sake of designing it to make other people understand it. It was a really fun topic and it was really paying attention the whole time, even though it wasn’t part of our syllabus it really helped me big time.
